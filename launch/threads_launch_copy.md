## First post

I ran the next OpenLine test.

The result is simple:

A healthy system is porous at the edge and strict at the core.

Let more signals into the buffer.
Let fewer claims alter protected state.
Make every crossing leave a receipt.

In the toy Ulam domain, wide intake + strict quorum beat porous direct admission cleanly.

This is the beginning of a cognitive immune layer for agents.

## Follow-up

The key distinction is intake vs. commit.

A weak system protects itself by refusing to look.

A stronger system can inspect more because it has a hardened write barrier.

That matters for AI agents, science workflows, organizations, and personal belief systems.

Open edges.
Strict commits.
Portable receipts.
